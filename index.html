THE HSL Presents

BuildWars

BuildWars is a series of hackathon featuring thematic challenges. Whether you're in for an in-depth build or thrive in fast-paced challenges, we have a fair challenge for you.
Grab your team with you and aim for the prize of ₦500,000!

Event Timeline

Initiation / Onboarding
March 31 - April 12th, 2025

Registration opens for the event. Participants receive detailed guidelines and rules, ensuring everyone is clear about expectations.
To help participants understand what the hackathon entails, an Open Round would be held on the 29th of March.
Participation in the Open Round is still restricted to registered individuals and teams.

Building & Qualifiers
April 26 - May 24, 2025 (Every Saturday)

Every Saturday, contestants showcase their progress in pitch competitions. Finalists are chosen through demos, pitch decks, and exhibits. Look out for workshops organized nearby.

Competition Final
Date TBD (To Be Determined)

After much improvement, finalists present their best projects developed over the duration of the hackathon over two stages: pitches and live demos. Judges evaluate each project, evaluating the market readiness and scalability of their products. The winning team receives ₦500,000. Additional prizes and certificates are also awarded.

Frequently Asked Questions

The hackathon is open to all students on campus. This implies that students currently on IT are exempt from the event. Teams can consist of 3-5 members, and individual participants will be matched to create balanced teams.

The requirement to participation is an issued ID card to have access to the Workspace in CUCRID Building.
This is issued only to HSL members who have paid their community dues for the semester. Visit the HSL Office on 3rd Floor, CUCRID Building for more details.

Individual participants are welcome. We will facilitate team matching to ensure you have the right mix of skills.
Special judging criteria would be announced to all participants as required.

Projects and tasks in each round would judged be based on creativity, technical complexity, and impact. Winners are determined weekly via a live leaderboard—more details coming soon.

We recommend you still take your school work as seriously as you would otherwise. For excursions and school recommended events, such as EYAC and Chaplaincy conferences, announcements would be made as necessary.
